What is Waste Segregation?

Waste segregation is the process of separating different types of waste materials for proper disposal or recycling. It involves categorizing waste into different bins or containers based on their composition and characteristics.

Importance of Waste Segregation

Proper waste segregation is crucial for several reasons:

  • Reduces environmental pollution
  • Conserves natural resources
  • Promotes recycling and reuse
  • Prevents health hazards
  • Complies with waste management regulations

How to Segregate Waste

Waste can be segregated into different categories such as:

  • Biodegradable waste
  • Recyclable waste
  • Non-recyclable waste

Tips for Effective Waste Segregation

  • Use separate bins for different types of waste
  • Label bins clearly
  • Educate and involve community members
  • Regularly review and update waste segregation practices
